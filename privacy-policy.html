Privacy Policy

Effective Date: September 23, 2025
Last Updated: September 23, 2025

1. Introduction

Dr. Randall Mercier M.D. ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy and maintaining the confidentiality of your personal health information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website randallmercier-md.com or receive medical services at our practice.

2. HIPAA Compliance

As a healthcare provider, we comply with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) and its Privacy Rule. We maintain separate Notice of Privacy Practices (NPP) documents that govern how we use and disclose your protected health information (PHI) in our medical practice. This Privacy Policy specifically addresses our website and online services.

3. Information We Collect

3.1 Personal Information

  • Contact information (name, address, phone number, email)
  • Appointment scheduling information
  • Insurance information
  • Medical history and health information (when provided through secure patient portals)
  • Emergency contact information

3.2 Website Usage Information

  • IP address and browser information
  • Pages visited and time spent on our website
  • Referring website information
  • Device and operating system information
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies

4. How We Use Your Information

We use your information for the following purposes:

  • Medical Treatment: Providing medical care, diagnosis, and treatment
  • Payment Processing: Billing and insurance claims
  • Healthcare Operations: Quality improvement and administrative functions
  • Appointment Management: Scheduling and appointment reminders
  • Communication: Health education and practice updates
  • Website Improvement: Analyzing usage to enhance user experience
  • Legal Compliance: Meeting regulatory and legal requirements

5. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We may share your information in the following circumstances:

  • Healthcare Providers: For coordinated care and referrals
  • Insurance Companies: For claims processing and authorization
  • Business Associates: Vendors who help us provide services (under HIPAA-compliant agreements)
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, court order, or regulatory authority
  • Emergency Situations: To prevent serious harm to health or safety
  • Public Health: For disease reporting and prevention activities

6. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical, administrative, and physical safeguards to protect your information:

  • SSL encryption for data transmission
  • Secure servers with access controls
  • Regular security assessments and updates
  • Staff training on privacy and security
  • Limited access on a need-to-know basis
  • Secure disposal of records when no longer needed

7. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Analyze website traffic and usage patterns
  • Improve website functionality and user experience
  • Provide relevant health information and news

You can control cookies through your browser settings, though this may limit some website functionality.

8. Third-Party Services

Our website may integrate with third-party services:

  • Google Fonts: For website typography
  • Font Awesome: For icons and graphics
  • News RSS Feeds: For health news content
  • Analytics Services: For website performance monitoring

These services may have their own privacy policies and data collection practices.

9. Patient Rights

Under HIPAA and applicable privacy laws, you have the right to:

  • Access and review your health information
  • Request corrections to inaccurate information
  • Request restrictions on use and disclosure
  • Request confidential communications
  • File complaints about privacy practices
  • Receive a copy of our Notice of Privacy Practices

10. Data Retention

We retain your information as required by:

  • Medical record retention laws (typically 7-10 years)
  • Insurance and billing requirements
  • Legal and regulatory obligations
  • Legitimate business needs

11. Minors and Children

Our website is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without parental consent. For pediatric patients, we follow applicable laws regarding parental authorization and minor consent.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in our practices or applicable laws. We will post the updated policy on our website with the effective date. Material changes will be communicated through appropriate channels.

13. State-Specific Rights

Depending on your state of residence, you may have additional privacy rights under state laws such as the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) or similar legislation. Please contact us for information about state-specific rights.
